Trip #1 Kanto + Kansai

Embark on a journey through the Kansai and Kanto regions of Japan, where ancient traditions blend seamlessly with modernity. Start in Osaka, known for its delicious street food and iconic castle. Next, head to Kyoto to explore the peaceful Arashiyama Bamboo Grove and the stunning Fushimi Inari Shrine. Continue your adventure by visiting Nara, home to the majestic Todai-ji Temple and friendly deer roaming freely in the nearby park. Travel further east to Tokyo, the bustling metropolis that never sleeps. Marvel at the towering skyscrapers, vibrant nightlife, and rich cultural heritage. Visit the serene Meiji Shrine, cross the famous Shibuya Crossing, and take a stroll through the Imperial Palace gardens. Finally, end your trip in Hakone, where you can relax in natural hot springs while enjoying the breathtaking views of Mount Fuji.

Trip #3 Okinawa

As an island group famous for robust centenarians, brutal Second World War battles and the bragging rights of being the healthiest place on earth, an Okinawa itinerary should certainly be considered if you’re travelling in Japan and have the gift of time on your side. Roughly 400 miles south of mainland Japan and 300 miles north of Taiwan, Okinawa’s isolation has given it the chance to develop a wholly unique culture, away from the norms of the West and in some ways, even Japan itself. It’s not a big place by any stretch of the imagination, but this one-week Okinawa itinerary leaves no stone unturned and will give you some insight into how to soak up the distinctive Okinawa culture.

Trip #4 Aomori

Qinglong Temple is located in the Aomori Bessard in Koyasan, Aomori, and is famous for its largest bronze seated statue in Japan. The Showa Buddha's head is inlaid with a jeweled crown, a pendant of jade pendants hangs on his chest, and bracelets and arm rings are on his arms. The majestic posture is majestic and breathtaking. In addition to admiring the Big Buddha, there are also experience activities such as sitting in meditation and tasting vegetarian food.
